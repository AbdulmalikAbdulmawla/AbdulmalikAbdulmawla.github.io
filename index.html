Mapping Active Frontages — Retail & Urban Form

Lead author · 13th Space Syntax Symposium, Bergen

A method for mapping street-level activity where it actually meets the city: the building frontage. Retail, service, and residential frontages in Weimar are linked across every scale of urban form — street, plot, block, building — for MAUP-aware spatial queries, gravity accessibility, and centrality analysis. Built on a parametric spatial-query pipeline (eCAADe 2022).

Read the paper →

DeCodingSpaces Toolbox

Contributing author · C# / Grasshopper

A free, open-source toolbox for the computational analysis and generation of street networks, plots, and buildings. I developed C# components integrating statistical analysis and socioeconomic indicators into parametric urban models. A collaboration of Bauhaus Weimar, ETH Zürich, AIT Vienna, and FCL Singapore (GPL v3).

About

I am a spatial-economic researcher and computational urban designer. My work examines urban structures in relation to local value creation, service economies, and resilience — empirically linking spatial configuration to economic dynamics in order to support sustainable urban and regional development.

Methodologically I work with GIS, network analysis, agent-based simulation, and data-driven methods (Python, R, C#). My PhD project investigates the spatial-economic dynamics of local production and service structures: “Spatial dependency of urban retail: detailed methods for positioning street-level activities” (supervisor: Prof. Dr. Reinhard König).

Across nearly two decades — from architectural practice in Egypt and the UAE to a decade of teaching and research in Germany, Egypt, Jordan, and Ethiopia — I have consistently built the tools that make analysis reproducible: from Grasshopper plug-ins for spatial data analysis to interactive web platforms for open-data exploration and evidence-based design.
